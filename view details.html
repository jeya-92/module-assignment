Crystal Growth and Characterization:
Investigate different methods for growing crystals and then characterize their structure and properties using techniques like X-ray diffraction.
Nanomaterials:
Explore the unique properties of nanomaterials, such as quantum dots or nanowires, and their potential applications in electronics or energy.
Two-Dimensional Materials:
Study the properties of materials like graphene or transition metal dichalcogenides, which exist in a single-atom-thick layer, and their potential for new electronic devices.
Spintronics:
Investigate the use of electron spin, in addition to charge, to store and process information in electronic devices.
Thin-Film Transistors:
Study the properties and behavior of thin-film transistors, which are used in displays and other electronic applications.
Organic Semiconductors:
Explore the use of organic materials in electronic devices and investigate their properties, such as charge transport and light emission.
